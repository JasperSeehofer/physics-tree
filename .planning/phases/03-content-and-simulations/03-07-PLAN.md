1. `## Motivation {#motivation}` - Why gravitational orbits matter. Cover Newton's unification of celestial and terrestrial mechanics. Kepler's empirical laws explained by one force law. Modern relevance: satellites, space missions, exoplanet detection.

2. `## Derivation {#derivation}` - Each step in `<div data-derivation-step="N">` tags:
   - Step 1: Newton's law of gravitation: $F = \frac{GMm}{r^2}$ directed radially inward
   - Step 2: Equation of motion in polar coordinates, centripetal acceleration $a_r = -\frac{GM}{r^2}$
   - Step 3: Conservation of angular momentum $L = mr^2\dot{\theta} = \text{const}$ (Kepler's second law)
   - Step 4: Effective potential $U_{\text{eff}}(r) = -\frac{GMm}{r} + \frac{L^2}{2mr^2}$, show energy diagram
   - Step 5: Orbital equation: $r(\theta) = \frac{L^2/(GMm^2)}{1 + e\cos\theta}$ -- conic section with eccentricity $e$
   - Step 6: Classify orbits by energy/eccentricity: circle ($e=0$), ellipse ($0 less than e less than 1$), parabola ($e=1$), hyperbola ($e greater than 1$)
   - Step 7: Kepler's third law derivation: $T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM}a^3$ for the semi-major axis $a$

   Include `::simulation[orbital]` directive after step 5 or 6 so students can explore orbits interactively. Add text like "Adjust the orbital speed below to see how eccentricity changes the trajectory from circular to elliptical to hyperbolic."

3. `## Intuition {#intuition}` - Newton's cannonball thought experiment (fire horizontally from mountaintop -- too slow falls, just right orbits, too fast escapes). Energy perspective: orbit shape depends on total energy vs effective potential.

4. `## Examples {#examples}` - 3-4 worked examples:
   - Calculate orbital speed for circular orbit at altitude 400 km (ISS)
   - Determine escape velocity from Earth's surface
   - Find period of a satellite in geostationary orbit
   - Calculate eccentricity of an orbit given speed at perihelion

5. `## Misconceptions {#misconceptions}` - At least 4 misconception directives using the `::misconception[statement]{reveal=explanation}` syntax:
   - "Astronauts in orbit are weightless because there is no gravity" -> reveal: gravity is nearly as strong at ISS altitude; they are in freefall
   - "Orbits are maintained by a force pushing outward (centrifugal force)" -> reveal: no outward force; the gravitational pull IS the centripetal force curving the path
   - "Higher orbit means faster speed" -> reveal: higher orbits have LOWER speed ($v \propto 1/\sqrt{r}$)
   - "An object needs continuous thrust to stay in orbit" -> reveal: orbits are inertial paths; no fuel needed to maintain a stable orbit
