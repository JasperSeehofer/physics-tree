**Per D-07 from CONTEXT.md: "Rich illustrated sections — custom flat vector SVG illustrations matching the botanical/Kurzgesagt aesthetic."**

Create `content/classical-mechanics/illustrations/` directory and generate 10 SVG files.

**Design system colors to use:**
- Background: `#0d0f14` (void) or transparent
- Primary shapes: `#4caf7d` (leaf-green), `#f4b942` (sun-amber)
- Accents: `#3fc8d4` (sky-teal), `#e8547a` (bloom-pink)
- Text/labels: `#f0f2f5` (petal-white), `#8892a4` (mist)
- Structural: `#252932` (bark-mid), `#1a1d24` (bark-dark)
- Font: Nunito (use `font-family="Nunito, sans-serif"`)

**Style: Kurzgesagt flat vector** — bold shapes, no gradients (use flat fills), clean geometric forms, rounded corners where appropriate, thick strokes (2-3px), minimal detail, generous whitespace.

**Each SVG should be:**
- Viewbox-based (no fixed width/height), recommended viewBox="0 0 800 400"
- Self-contained inline SVGs (no external references)
- Clean, well-commented SVG markup
- Dark background or transparent (works on void background)

**Illustrations to create:**

1. **projectile-diagram.svg** — Parabolic trajectory arc with labeled angle, velocity vector arrows at launch, apex, and landing. Ground line. Dotted vertical and horizontal component lines.

2. **pendulum-energy.svg** — Three pendulum positions (left, center, right) showing energy bar charts below each: KE vs PE. Arrows showing energy conversion flow.

3. **newtons-laws-trio.svg** — Three panels side by side: (1) Object at rest with equal arrows, (2) F=ma with acceleration arrow, (3) Action-reaction pair with opposing arrows on two objects.

4. **incline-forces.svg** — Block on an inclined plane with labeled force vectors: gravity (straight down), normal force (perpendicular to surface), friction (parallel to surface, up-slope), weight components (parallel and perpendicular).

5. **orbital-paths.svg** — Central body (large circle, sun-amber) with three orbital paths: circular (solid green), elliptical (dashed teal), escape trajectory (dotted pink). Velocity vectors at key points.

6. **shm-spring.svg** — Spring-mass system in three states: compressed, equilibrium, extended. Below: sinusoidal displacement-vs-time curve. Arrows showing restoring force direction.

7. **momentum-collision.svg** — Before/after collision: two objects with velocity arrows and mass labels. Total momentum bars showing conservation. Both elastic and inelastic outcomes shown.

8. **circular-forces.svg** — Object on a circular path with centripetal acceleration arrow pointing inward, velocity tangent arrow, and radius labeled. Multiple positions around the circle showing changing velocity direction.

9. **kinematics-graphs.svg** — Three stacked mini-graphs: position vs time (parabola), velocity vs time (linear), acceleration vs time (constant). Connected by "derivative" arrows between them.

10. **work-energy-area.svg** — Force vs displacement graph with shaded area representing work done. Two scenarios: constant force (rectangle) and varying force (curve with shaded area).
