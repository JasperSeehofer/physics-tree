## Spaced Prompt

Without looking at your notes, write down all three kinematic equations for constant acceleration.

For each equation, state:
1. Which three of the five quantities ($x$, $x_0$, $v_0$, $v$, $a$, $t$) it connects.
2. One physical situation where you would reach for that equation first.

Then derive the third equation ($v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x$) from the first two — no looking at Phase 2. Can you reproduce the algebra from memory?

## Interleaving Problem

A rocket sled starts from rest and accelerates at $a = 20\,\text{m/s}^2$ along a horizontal track. After a distance of $\Delta x = 500\,\text{m}$, the engine cuts off and the sled coasts (zero acceleration). A braking system then applies a constant deceleration and brings the sled to rest after an additional $\Delta x = 250\,\text{m}$.

**(A) Kinematics:** Find the speed of the sled when the engine cuts off. Find the deceleration applied by the braking system.

**(B) Vector reasoning:** The problem is one-dimensional, but suppose the track curves upward at the end at an angle $\theta = 30°$ to the horizontal. If the sled's speed at the base of the curve is the speed you found in part (A), and you want to find how far along the incline the sled travels before stopping due to gravity alone (ignoring friction), which component of $g$ is the relevant acceleration? Set up the equation (you do not need to solve it fully).

*This problem combines constant-acceleration kinematics (this node) with vector decomposition (`vectors`). The two-stage structure also requires applying kinematics twice in sequence — a common pattern in real problems.*
