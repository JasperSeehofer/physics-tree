
```quiz
type: fill_in_formula
prompt: 'Write the kinematic equation for displacement as a function of time, starting from position $x_0$ with initial velocity $v_0$ and constant acceleration $a$.'
answer: 'x = x_0 + v_0*t + (1/2)*a*t^2'
difficulty: remember
```

## Transfer Problem

A diver stands on a 10 m platform above the water and jumps *upward* with an initial speed of $2.0\,\text{m/s}$. Take upward as positive and $g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$.

**(a)** What is the maximum height above the water that the diver reaches?

**(b)** How long after the jump does the diver hit the water?

**(c)** At what speed does the diver enter the water?

**Guidance:** Choose a coordinate system clearly — where is $x = 0$? What are the signs of $v_0$, $a$, and the final displacement $x - x_0$? The diver hits the water at a *lower* position than the platform, so the displacement from the platform is negative. Work through each part using the appropriate kinematic equation. Check that the speed in part (c) is greater than what free-fall alone from 10 m would give (it should be, because the diver first gained height then fell a larger total distance).

**Answers:** (a) $10.2\,\text{m}$ above the water; (b) $t \approx 1.85\,\text{s}$; (c) $v \approx 14.2\,\text{m/s}$
